Manifesto

  • account_balanceEllo's Manifesto

    Your social network is owned by advertisers.

    Every post you share, every friend you make and every link you follow is tracked, recorded and converted into data. Advertisers buy your data so they can show you more ads. You are the product that's bought and sold.

    We believe there is a better way. We believe in audacity. We believe in beauty, simplicity and transparency. We believe that the people who make things and the people who use them should be in partnership.

    We believe a social network can be a tool for empowerment. Not a tool to deceive, coerce and manipulate - but a place to connect, create and celebrate life.

    You are not a product.

  • commentA nobody's humble opinion

    The creators of Ello are bold and daring - I'll give them that. Being able to develop and maintain a social network without ads is a large feat, something they've been able to do since they've shared the social network to the outside of their litle artists circle. Let's face it, ads appearing left, right, and center - totally annoying BUT they do generate income. And income is something needed to keep a website, and a social network at that, up and running. In lieu of the creators allowing ads to infest their beautiful & ad-free social network, they've come up with different products (such as shirts and phone cases) and put them up for sale to take care of their bills *seems like it is doing its job just fine*. However, I still wonder until when they can keep ads out of the website...
