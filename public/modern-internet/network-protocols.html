Network Protocols

The process of sending and receiving information is quite complicated but hey, it works, it's fast, and it's efficient so why not? :)
Anyway, the first thing that happens when you're sending a file: the file gets broken down into pieces - packets - and are then sent separately. Packets are sent using either circuit switching or packet switching. Circuit switching is basically all the packets following the same route to the destination while packet switching involves the packets going through different routes. After this, the TCP-IP protocol does its job:

IP packages information such as the addressess of the sender and receipient while the LAN (Local Area Network) connects local networks. A proxy server may also exist, which acts as the middleman for Internet connection and security.
User Datagram Protocol (UDP) is lightweight and connectionless, with smaller packet sizes and control over the dissemination of data | message-oriented
The Transmission Control Protocol (TCP) is reliable and connection-based, making use of the three-way handshake - first PC establishes or requests for connection > first PC sends SYN (synchronization request) packet then the other PC sends an ACK (acknowledgement) packet; it also has congestion control that delays transmission when the network is congested | stream-oriented
Hub, Switch, and Routerswork on a different layer. The hubs are on the physical layer, dealing with raw data; switches, on the link layer, take care of the the mac addresses; and routers, being in the network layer, are in charge of the IP addresses.
