Video Streaming

Just like how information is broken down into packets, video files are also chopped into pieces which are sent one after the other to the receiving end. Such method is necessary because video files are immense. Just so you know, streaming is time-based and multi-processed, so it contains 3 layers of data management - encoded bits (aac anyone?), a container that holds the bits together (mp4, for example), and a transport that moves the stream from the media server to the player. M3U8 files exist - which is a type of special file - responsible for telling the player the specific order in which to play the video.
