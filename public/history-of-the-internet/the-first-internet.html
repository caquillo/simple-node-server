The First Internet

Back in 1962, scientist J.C.R. Licklider proposed a network of computers that could talk to one another. Over the ARPANET, the first message was sent from one computer (UCLA) to another (Stanford). However, the network crashed before the whole word 'login' was sent, making the first word ever to be sent just 'lo'. By the end of the year, 4 computers were connected to that network. While the network continued to grow, what with more and more universities from America and other neighboring countries, Ray Tomlinson, in 1971, developed a system to send mail back and forth between users of ARPANET which eventually became known as email. The use of the symbol '@' also started at that time.Through time, non-universities were allowed to use the network. On the 28th of Feb, 1990, the ARPANET finally retired but the Internet lived on.
