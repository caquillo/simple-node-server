Early World Wide Web

A year to remember: 1990s | That's when it all began.

Note that the Internet and the Web are two very different things - the Internet connects participants while the Web connects information (hypertext documents).

You could say1991 was when Tim Berners-Lee officiated the marraige of hypertexts and the Internet to produce a web that was worldwide = world wide web. It all started when he wrote a paper (Information Management:A Proposal) that proposed the usage of a 'web' instead of a fixed hierarchal system of information.
1992 was the dawn of the browsers, an Internet browser called 'Erwise' was the first to have a graphical interphase. Mosaic, in 1993, popularized 'surfing the web'.
Nowadays, there are a lot of Internet browsers with Google Chrome, Safari, and Firefox being the most popular.
